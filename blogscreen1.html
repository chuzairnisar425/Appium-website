Emerging Interaction Technologies

In today's digital paradigm, technology is advancing by leaps and bounds. It's not very long ago when we used bulky keyboards, pixelated screens and button-equipped mobile phones. Then, in 2007 Apple introduced the first touch screen mobile and everyone was in awe (Forbes). But today technology has taken a new turn that has completely transformed the way humans interact with machines. It's none other than Emerging Interaction Technologies. In this article we will discuss the miracles of this technology and how it is going to impact the user experience with computers or machines. Emerging Interaction Technologies: Emerging Interaction Technologies involves all those continually developing technologies that have transformed human-machine interaction. These include the following:

  • Virtual Reality (VR)
  • Augmented Reality(AR)
  • Gesture Recognition Haptics
  • Brain-Computer interactions

Virtual Reality (VR): Have you ever imagined yourself diving into the sea to touching the coral reefs teeming or climbing the gigantic peak of Mount Everest? With virtual reality technology, you can do that from the comfort of your lounge. This VR technology is developed in a way that with specially designed headsets, you can experience the virtual world with a natural sense of presence. Moreover, you can also explore and interact wi th the virtual environment. By wearing these headsets, you can become part of the movie, game or whatever you want to see and enjoy. This technology has also been employed in different domains like education, training, and virtual travel. Fortunately, these headsets are becoming more affordable and convenient to use.
Augmented Reality (AR) Virtual reality takes you to another world but with Augmented Reality, you can overlay digital information and graphics onto the real world. This technology has endless possibilities. You can enjoy this feature on your smartphone, AR glasses, headsets, projectors, etc. Depending upon your choice, you can enjoy games by overlaying the gaming scenario or visualizing a 3D design of a historical landmark. In addition, if you wear AR glasses, it can display turn-by-turn navigation while walking on the road. Snapchat is one of the most common examples of the AR that overlays a filter on the picture or video of the user. In other words, AR superimposes digital information onto the physical world while creating a natural blend of the two. Gesture Recognition: Imagine controlling your devices with simple hand movements and navigating your smart device menus with facial expressions is yet another miracle of emerging interaction technologies. With a touch less user interface, this sophisticated technology uses body and face gestures to perform tasks. It has potential to transform the way humans interact with machines. Gesture recognition technology can be used to control VR and AR devices. It can be also be used in smartphones, automobiles, healthcare and many other sectors.
Haptics: Haptic technology gives you a feeling of a real experience of touch in the form of vibration pressure or weight. It actually creates tactile feedback, allowing users to feel virtual objects. Haptics are used in VR and AR technologies to enhance the immersive experience which ultimately make the interactions of humans with computers and machines more realistic and engaging
Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs): It’s not a Holly wood movie where you could control machine with your thoughts, it’s today’s Brain-Computer interaction where you can control devices with your thoughts. The technology is still in stages of development. Experiments are being performed on animals like monkeys have been used to navigate the computer by using thinking as a command. Even similar results have been obtained during research in Japan. So that time is near when we will be looking at its implementation in real world.
The Human Touch: Balancing Technology with Social Interaction While emerging technologies offer exciting possibilities, it's important to remember the importance of human connection. As we dig deeper into the digital world, creating meaningful real-world interactions remains crucial. The goal is to utilize these technologies to enhance, not replace, the human experience.
The Future is now: Embracing the Possibilities The future of human interaction is brimming with possibilities. Emerging technologies like VR and AR promise to transform the way we live, work, and learn. By embracing these advancements thoughtfully, we can create a future where technology empowers us to connect, explore, and interact with the world around us. This is in ways never imagined before
